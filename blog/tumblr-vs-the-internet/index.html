Tumblr vs. the Internet
23 May 2013

This past week’s tech news has mostly been about Yahoo buying Tumblr for $1 billion. It makes sense – Yahoo (with Marissa Mayer) is trying to become hip again, and Tumblr is running out of money due to a unprofitable business model.

Tumblr feels like Facebook and Twitter – it has that clean and simple tech startup design, it’s funded by venture capital, and it’s frequently discussed in the Silicon Valley context1. But Tumblr isn’t that kind of social network. It’s not like Facebook or Twitter where the site is mostly a real life friend network; instead, it’s a network of common interests and internet subcultures.

Yahoo is buying a blogging platform with Tumblr, but they’re not buying a real social network attached to it. They’re getting something more akin to message boards and comment sections – online community websites such as DeviantART, 4chan, Reddit, etc. – that are for the most part controlled by a faceless userbase, one where the users have no greater connection to each other than, for example, a love of Doctor Who2. And it’s always nice for that to exist.

It’s strange to see the “outside world” gain an investment in these kinds of sites3, but it’s somewhat inevitable. Still, what’s the point of buying a community like this without screwing it up? Let’s see what happens.
