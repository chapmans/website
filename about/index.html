About

My name is Chapman Seto. I am originally from San Francisco, though I'm currently a third year Computer Science major at UC San Diego. My primary interest (and hopeful career) is in computers and programming – to see what I've worked on, take a look at my resume and GitHub account. I'm interested in the unforseen ways that technology can impact anyone's lives, especially with the idea that I can still have access to something to connect to others with if I'm stranded out in the middle of nowhere. Computers are just containers of data, but it's interesting how much can be done with them.

I have also been interested in design. It's often the little things in life that can be confusing, whether its a ambiguous door handle or an indistinguishability between "I"s and "l"s. As a developer, it's important to know the context of my code, and ideas from design help with that.

Other things about me: I'm okay with hiking. I'm pretty much lukewarm on video games. I'm in the "The Wire is the best TV Show ever" group. I dislike watching Youtube videos because I know exactly how much time I'm going to waste watching one. The Books' The Lemon of Pink is a brilliant album; Belle & Sebastian's If You're Feeling Sinister is pretty great too.
